About Me

Attorney, Economist, and Public Servant

I am a dual Argentine-American attorney, economist, and public servant dedicated to building stronger, more equitable communities. My career spans community development, municipal policy, and legal advocacy, with a focus on housing, economic development, and urban revitalization.

As Executive Director of the City of St. Louis Community Development Administration since September 2022, I lead a team of forty professionals and have tripled the agency's investments from 2022 to 2025 without any audit findings. I oversee hundreds of legal agreements with public and private partners and guide the City's consolidated planning process, vacancy strategy, and housing initiatives.

In August 2025, I joined Washington University School of Law as an Adjunct Professor, teaching Community Development Law and introducing students to the federal programs, local government processes, and diverse contractual agreements that shape community development at the local level.
